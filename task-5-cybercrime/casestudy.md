**What the Crime Is:**
The "Receive Money" scam is a prevalent form of UPI/Online Payment Fraud. It is a social engineering deception where cybercriminals trick users into authorizing a UPI payment out of their account, under the false pretense that the user is actually *receiving* money for goods or services.

**How it Typically Happens (Step-by-Step):**
1. **The Bait:** A college student lists an item for sale (e.g., an old textbook or laptop) on a classifieds platform like OLX or a campus Facebook group.
2. **The Hook:** A scammer, posing as an eager buyer, contacts the student immediately. They agree to the asking price without any negotiation to create excitement. 
3. **The Excuse:** The "buyer" claims they cannot pay in cash—often citing they are military personnel, out of town, or in a rush—and insists on paying immediately via Google Pay, PhonePe, or Paytm.
4. **The Trap:** The scammer sends a QR code or a payment request link via WhatsApp. They instruct the student to scan the code or click the link and enter their UPI PIN, falsely claiming this step is required to "receive the funds" securely.
5. **The Theft:** Unaware that a UPI PIN is exclusively used for *sending* money, the student enters their PIN. The specified amount is instantly deducted from the student's linked bank account and transferred to the scammer.


6. **Who is Usually Targeted:**
This scam heavily targets college students selling used items, gig workers, and individuals who are somewhat new to the technical nuances of digital payment platforms.


**The Consequences:**
The immediate consequence is a sudden financial loss, which can be devastating for a student on a tight budget—often leaving them unable to pay for tuition, rent, or daily expenses. Secondary consequences include severe anxiety, a loss of trust in digital payment systems, and the stressful, often lengthy process of attempting to recover the funds through bank disputes.
