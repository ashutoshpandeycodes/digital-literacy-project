##Stay Safe Online: Prevention Checklist

A guide for college students to navigate the digital world securely.

## General Online Safety
1. **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA):** Turn on 2FA for all critical accounts, especially your primary email, social media, and university portals.
2. **Use Strong, Unique Passwords:** Never use your phone number, pet's name, or date of birth. Use a password manager to maintain different, complex passwords for every site.
3. **Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Sensitive Tasks:** Never log into your bank account or pay fees over free, unsecured campus or cafe Wi-Fi networks.
4. **Think Before You Click:** Be highly skeptical of unsolicited links offering free OTT subscriptions, heavy electronics discounts, or "urgent" warnings about your accounts.
5. **Verify Urgent Requests from Friends:** If a friend messages you urgently asking for financial help, call them directly to verify. Their account may have been hacked.
6. **Keep Software Updated:** Regularly update your phone's OS, apps, and antivirus software to patch known security vulnerabilities.

## Financial & UPI Safety
7. **Never Enter PIN to Receive Money:** Remember the golden rule of UPI: You only need to enter your UPI PIN to *send* money or check your balance. You NEVER need a PIN to receive funds.
8. **Do Not Scan Strangers' QR Codes:** Refuse to scan QR codes sent by unknown buyers on WhatsApp or online marketplaces to complete a transaction.

## What to do if you are compromised?
If you fall victim to a cybercrime or financial fraud, report it immediately:
* **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal:** Visit [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in/)
* **Emergency Cybercrime Helpline:** Dial **1930**
