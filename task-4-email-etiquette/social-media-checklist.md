# Social Media Do's and Don'ts Checklist

A quick reference guide for responsible social media use as a college student.

## The Do's (Best Practices)
* **Do review your privacy settings:** Regularly check who can see your posts, photos, and personal information across all platforms.
* **Do build a professional presence:** Use platforms like LinkedIn to highlight your academic projects, skills, and career aspirations.
* **Do think before you post:** Assume everything you post is permanent and could be seen by future employers, professors, or family.
* **Do engage respectfully:** Disagree politely in comments and foster constructive digital conversations rather than resorting to personal attacks.
* **Do verify information before sharing:** Fact-check news articles and announcements to avoid spreading misinformation to your network.

---

## The Don'ts (Pitfalls to Avoid)
* **Don't post unprofessional content:** Avoid sharing photos or texts depicting illegal activities, excessive partying, or inappropriate behavior.
* **Don't badmouth peers, professors, or past employers:** Public venting can severely damage your reputation and bridge-burn valuable connections.
* **Don't overshare sensitive personal details:** Keep your home address, phone number, and real-time location private to protect your physical and digital security.
* **Don't engage with internet trolls:** Avoid getting drawn into endless, heated arguments that only drain your energy and leave a negative digital footprint.
* **Don't rely solely on social media for news:** Avoid taking headlines at face value; always cross-reference important information with reputable sources.
